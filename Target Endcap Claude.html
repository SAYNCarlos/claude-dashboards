The March Endcap drove ~$865K in incremental revenue

Endcap stores sold $3.18M during the event — but ~$2.31M of that was growth they'd have earned anyway, given seasonality, year-over-year market growth, and the new product launch (measured from the non-endcap control stores). The endcap's true contribution is the gap above that counterfactual.

Where the lift came from

Waterfall from the expected (counterfactual) baseline to actual sales, broken out by product. Every dollar of lift comes from the four newly launched SKUs; the two legacy "OG" products ran slightly below baseline — a small cannibalization drag.

Why raw year-over-year misleads

Sales per store, same Mar–May window. The two groups started 2026 nearly identical (~$2,600/store) — then endcap stores pulled far ahead. Both grew because new SKUs launched chain-wide; only the gap is the endcap.

It's a launch story

The endcap was the launch vehicle. In endcap stores the four new SKUs — SPF0, Turbo, and the two Minis — sold 83–122% more per store than in control stores.

Legacy products dipped slightly (Intensive OG −$60K, Shimmer OG −$25K vs. baseline), suggesting the display shifted attention toward the new line.

Robust, not noise. Median endcap store grew 1.88× vs. 1.39× for controls (Mann–Whitney p ≈ 10⁻⁹¹).

A conservative floor of ~$780K applies if you credit the ~3.7% faster pre-event trend in endcap stores to something other than the event.

Method: difference-in-differences on a matched panel of stores selling in both years, comparing the identical Mar 1–May 31 window across 2025 and 2026. The chain-wide Circle BOGO promo covers both groups near-uniformly and nets out. Counterfactual = each SKU's 2025 endcap-store sales grown at the control group's SKU-level YoY rate; new SKUs benchmarked to control per-store rates.
