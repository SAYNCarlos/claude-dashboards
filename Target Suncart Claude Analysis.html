The Suncart SKUs doubled the week the event launched — but how much is "incremental" can't be pinned down yet

The event runs 5/10–8/1/2026 at all stores on four SKUs; data covers the first 43 of 84 days. Because it's chain-wide, there's no control group of untreated stores — so I used the two SKUs not in the event (the Minis) as a within-store control. They tracked the event SKUs almost perfectly until 5/10, then split apart.

⚠
Why a single incremental number would mislead. Two of the four event SKUs (SPF0, Turbo) launched in 2026 and have no prior-year baseline, so their surge mixes the event with an active launch ramp. And full-size sun-care has stronger summer seasonality than the Mini control, which inflates a naive comparison. The reliable, own-baseline estimate covers only the two legacy SKUs.

How to read the range

Floor (~$238K): measuring the two legacy SKUs against their own 2025 summer, the event softened their year-over-year decline — a modest, defensible gain.

Ceiling (~$1.96M): a naive cross-SKU comparison credits the event for the full gap vs the Minis. That almost certainly overstates it, since full-size products ramp into summer far harder than travel Minis regardless of any event.

The honest read: the reliably-attributable amount so far is ~$238K, plus a likely-but-unprovable contribution from SPF0 and Turbo. The new SKUs sold $1.65M during the window, but no prior-year data exists to say how much of that is the event vs the launch itself.

Recommendation: re-run at event close (8/1), and — most useful — hold out a small control group of stores next time so incrementality is measurable on the new SKUs too.

Method: cross-SKU within-2026 difference-in-differences (control = the two non-event Mini SKUs), validated by tight pre-event parallel trends. The reliable estimate instead uses a within-product year-over-year acceleration test on the legacy SKUs (counterfactual = 2025 event-window sales grown at each product's own pre-event YoY rate), which captures their true summer seasonality. Figures are partial through 6/21/2026; data combines the store CSV with the L3D Excel extension (6/19–6/21).
