How to read it

The good: $24.6K in five weeks with sales-per-store above the mainland, and a clear acceleration once the endcap went live. In absolute terms these stores are performing.

The caution: a fixed-window comparison against the mainland's steep summer ramp (+95%) puts Hawaii roughly in line or ~9% below — so depending on where you draw the baseline, the endcap-specific lift swings from slightly negative to clearly positive. With 10 new stores and half the window, that spread is the honest answer.

Recommendation: re-run at event close (7/11) when the full window is in and the stores have matured another two months. That will tighten the estimate far more than any modeling can now.

Method: within-2026 interrupted-time-series. Year-over-year DiD (used for the mainland endcap) is not possible here — 8 of 10 Hawaii stores have no 2025 sales. Counterfactual ramp estimated from 1,338 non-Hawaii, non-event stores over identical calendar windows; figures are partial (through 6/18/2026) and the mainland seasonal ramp may overstate Hawaii's, biasing the isolated lift downward.
