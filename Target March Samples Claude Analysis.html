The Sampling event gave a ~$9K one-week nudge — then it faded

A single-day in-store sampling at 37 stores (excluding 13 that also ran the endcap). The sampling stores were already ~2× the size of typical stores, so only their growth relative to control is meaningful. There was a genuine bump the week of the event, but it decayed to nothing within a month and isn't statistically robust.

Read with caution

The typical store saw no lift. Median sampling-store growth was 1.37× vs. 1.42× for control — the bump comes from a few stores, not broad trial conversion (Mann–Whitney p ≈ 0.50).

Why the range? Sampling stores historically grow at ~85% of control's rate. Hold them to control's rate and the 4-week net is ~$5K; credit their slower baseline and it's ~$13K. The spread reflects how thin the signal is.

Bottom line: a real but minor, short-lived trial effect — useful for awareness, not a revenue driver on its own.
